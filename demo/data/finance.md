### Executive Summary
Jokes Company is a **global entertainment and merchandise brand** that creates, distributes, and licenses comedic content across digital platforms, live events, and consumer products. For the fiscal year ended **31 December 2025**, the company delivered **strong revenue growth** driven by a surge in streaming subscriptions, a successful global tour, and the launch of a new line of novelty merchandise.

- **Revenue** grew **23 %** YoY to **$14.8 bn**.  
- **EBITDA** improved to **$3.2 bn**, reflecting operating leverage.  
- **Net income** reached **$1.7 bn**, a **28 %** increase over FY 2026.  
- **Free cash flow** stood at **$1.4 bn**, supporting a dividend increase and share repurchase program.

The financial results underscore Jokes Company’s **robust business model**, diversified revenue streams, and **capacity to generate cash** for shareholders while continuing to invest in content creation and platform expansion.

## Notes to the Financial Statements  

1. **Basis of Presentation** – The statements have been prepared in accordance with **U.S. GAAP** and are for informational purposes only.  
2. **Revenue Recognition** – Revenue is recognized when control of the promised service or product transfers to the customer, consistent with ASC 606.  
   - **Streaming subscriptions** (45 % of total revenue) are recognized ratably over the subscription period.  
   - **Merchandise sales** are recognized at the point of sale.  
3. **Intangible Assets** – Consist mainly of **content libraries, trademarks, and goodwill** arising from the acquisition of “LaughLab Studios” (2026). Goodwill is tested for impairment annually; no impairment was recorded in 2025.  
4. **Share-Based Compensation** – Stock‑based awards are measured at fair value on the grant date using the Black‑Scholes model. The expense is amortized over the vesting period (average 3 years).  
5. **Acquisition Details** – In Q3 2025, Jokes Company purchased a **15 % non‑controlling interest** in **ComedyTech Ltd.** for **$250 m**, funded by a **pari‑passu term loan**. The investment is accounted for using the equity method; the investor’s share of net income was **$30 m** (recorded in “Other Income”).  
6. **Income Taxes** – Effective tax rate of **30 %** reflects statutory rates, deferred tax assets, and valuation allowances.  
7. **Dividends & Share Repurchases** – The board authorized a **dividend of $1.38 per share** (15 % increase) and a **$500 m share‑repurchase plan** for FY 2026.

## Management Discussion & Outlook  

### 1. Business Performance  
- **Streaming Platform**: Subscriber base grew to **35 million** (up 22 % YoY). The launch of the “Jokes + Premium” tier added $1.1 bn in recurring revenue.  
- **Live Events**: The “World Laugh Tour” generated $1.3 bn in ticket sales, surpassing expectations due to **post‑pandemic demand**.  
- **Merchandising**: Novelty items (e.g., “Laugh‑in‑a‑Box” kits) contributed $1.7 bn, reflecting successful **cross‑channel promotions**.  

### 2. Strategic Initiatives  
| Initiative | Status | Expected Impact (FY 2026) |
|------------|--------|---------------------------|
| **Expansion into Asia‑Pacific** | New regional office opened in Singapore; local content production underway. | +$800 m revenue, +5 % margin |
| **AI‑Driven Content Personalisation** | Beta testing with 10 % of subscribers. | Incremental ARPU +$1.50 |
| **ComedyTech Integration** | Joint development of interactive comedy‑gaming platform. | New revenue stream, $200 m in FY 2025 |
| **Sustainability Programme** | Target 30 % reduction in Scope 1‑2 emissions by 2026. | ESG rating improvement, access to green financing |

### 3. Outlook for FY 2026  
- **Revenue growth** of **18‑22 %** is projected, driven by continued **subscriber acquisition** and the **Asia‑Pacific rollout**.  
- **EBITDA margin** expected to reach **22‑23 %** as operating leverage improves.  
- **Capital expenditures** will be capped at **$550 m** to fund technology upgrades and the ComedyTech joint venture.  

Management reaffirmed the **2025 targets**: revenue of **$22 bn**, dividend yield of **4 %**, and a **share‑repurchase budget** of **$800 m**.
