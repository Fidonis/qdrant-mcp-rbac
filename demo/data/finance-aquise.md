## 2. Target Overview

### 2.1 Company Profile

Gelotopia S.A. was founded in 2018 by two former stand-up comedians, **Rodrigo Fuentealba** and **Astrid Ljung**, initially as a podcast network. It pivoted to video streaming in 2020 and launched its live-event ticketing integration in 2022. Key facts:

- **Headquarters**: Barcelona, Spain (satellite offices in Berlin, Warsaw, Amsterdam)
- **Employees**: 640 FTE (≈ 280 in content / creator partnerships)
- **Subscribers**: 22.4 million (paid); 8.1 million (ad-supported free tier)
- **Content library**: 4,200+ titles (originals + licensed); exclusive deal with **ComedyUnion Europe** (200 touring comedians)
- **Platform**: Proprietary streaming stack; iOS, Android, Smart TV, Web
- **Revenue model**: Subscription (68 %) · Advertising (18 %) · Live-event ticketing (14 %)

## 4. Due Diligence Findings

### 4.1 Financial DD (Ironwood Partners LLC — preliminary)

- **Revenue quality**: 94 % of FY 2026 revenue is recurring or contracted. ✅
- **Churn**: Monthly subscriber churn at 1.8 % — below European streaming median of 2.4 %. ✅
- **Working capital**: Negative working capital cycle (subscribers pay upfront). Net cash position € 38 m. ✅
- **Off-balance-sheet items**: Two operating leases (Barcelona HQ, Berlin office) not yet IFRS-16 reflected in management accounts. Adjusts EBITDA down by ≈ € 4 m. ⚠ — within materiality threshold.
- **Related-party transactions**: Founders Fuentealba & Ljung hold a jointly-owned production entity **Banana Films S.L.** that licenses content to Gelotopia on a cost-plus 12 % basis. Total volume FY 2026: € 6.2 m. **Must be unwound at close** or arm's-length pricing certified by independent auditor. 🔴 **Open item — legal review in progress.**

### 4.2 Legal DD (Whitmore & Pfeiffer — preliminary)

- **IP ownership**: 91 % of original content is fully owned by Gelotopia. Remaining 9 % carries residual licensor reversion rights if Gelotopia is acquired by a "competitor." Definition of "competitor" in those agreements is ambiguous. **Counsel is seeking clarification; worst-case exposure ≈ € 12 m content write-off.** ⚠
- **Employment**: Spanish collective bargaining agreement applies to 310 employees. Change-of-control clause triggers enhanced severance equivalent to 3 months' salary if triggered within 12 months post-close. Estimated liability if activated in full: ≈ € 8.2 m. ⚠
- **Regulatory**: Notification required under Spain's FDI screening regime (COFIDES) and EU Merger Regulation (turnover thresholds met in 3 member states). Timeline: 4–5 months. No substantive competition concerns anticipated — Jokes Company has < 1 % share in European comedy streaming today.
- **Data privacy**: GDPR compliance audit completed by Gelotopia's DPO in 2026 — no material findings. Data processing agreements with third-party ad-tech vendors require refreshing post-close. ✅ (manageable)

### 4.3 Technical & Product DD (internal JC Engineering team)

- Platform is microservices-based (Kubernetes / GCP). Compatible with JC's existing infrastructure. ✅
- Recommendation engine runs on a proprietary ML model. **Model weights and training data are NOT being sold** — they are retained by a Luxembourg IP holdco controlled by the founders. **This is a significant omission.** 🔴 Deal team has requested re-negotiation; founders are resistant. Current fallback: 36-month data-sharing and model-access licence.
- Mobile app ratings: iOS 4.6 ⭐ / Android 4.3 ⭐ — strong. ✅

### 4.4 People & Culture DD

- Founders plan to **exit within 18 months** post-close. Retention packages under negotiation (€ 4 m each, 2-year vest). ⚠ — Key-man risk flagged.
- Content team has high internal NPS (82). Risk of poaching by competitors post-announcement. HR recommends immediate retention bonus pool (est. € 6 m). ⚠
- **Confidential finding (do not share with target)**: Three senior engineers have active LinkedIn profiles indicating job searches. Escalated to integration planning team.
